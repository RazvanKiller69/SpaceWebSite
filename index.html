Fun Facts

  • Space is Silent: Unlike what movies often portray, space is mostly silent because sound waves require a medium like air to travel through.
  • Neutron stars are incredibly dense remnants of massive stars that have exploded in supernovae. A sugar-cube-sized amount of neutron star material would weigh about a billion tons on Earth.
  • In space, water can exist in a state where it appears to float as small blobs due to surface tension. Astronauts have to be careful with water droplets as they can float away unpredictably.
  • There are millions of pieces of space junk orbiting Earth, including old satellites, rocket stages, and debris from collisions. This junk poses a risk to operational spacecraft.
  • Mars has polar ice caps composed largely of water ice and dry ice (frozen carbon dioxide). Some regions of Mars have even revealed evidence of past lakes that could have sustained microbial life.
  • According to Einstein's theory of relativity, time moves slower in stronger gravitational fields. This means astronauts on the International Space Station age slightly slower than people on Earth due to the station's orbital speed and altitude.
